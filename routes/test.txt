<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" style="margin:0.5em; background:#cef2e0; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:0.1em 0.6em;"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Buckingham_cropped.png" class="image" title="Stipple engraved of Humphrey Stafford, 1st Duke of Buckingham by William Bond, published by Philip Yorke, published 1 August 1798"><img alt="Stipple engraved of Humphrey Stafford, 1st Duke of Buckingham by William Bond, published by Philip Yorke, published 1 August 1798" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Buckingham_cropped.png/120px-Buckingham_cropped.png" decoding="async" width="120" height="144" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Buckingham_cropped.png/180px-Buckingham_cropped.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Buckingham_cropped.png/240px-Buckingham_cropped.png 2x" data-file-width="328" data-file-height="394" /></a>
</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Humphrey_Stafford,_1st_Duke_of_Buckingham" title="Humphrey Stafford, 1st Duke of Buckingham">Humphrey Stafford, 1st Duke of Buckingham</a></b> (1402&#160;– 10 July 1460) was an English nobleman and a military commander who fought for the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lancaster" title="House of Lancaster">Lancastrian</a> <a href="/wiki/Henry_VI_of_England" title="Henry VI of England">King Henry&#160;VI</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Wars_of_the_Roses" title="Wars of the Roses">Wars of the Roses</a>, where he was killed at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Northampton_(1460)" title="Battle of Northampton (1460)">Battle of Northampton</a>. Through his mother he had <a href="/wiki/Royal_descent" title="Royal descent">royal blood</a> as a great-grandson of <a href="/wiki/Edward_III_of_England" title="Edward III of England">King Edward&#160;III</a>, and from his father, he inherited the <a href="/wiki/Baron_Stafford" title="Baron Stafford">earldom of Stafford</a>. He joined the English campaign in France with <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_of_England" title="Henry V of England">King Henry&#160;V</a> in 1420. Following the king's death two years later, he became a councillor for the nine-month-old <a href="/wiki/Henry_VI_of_England" title="Henry VI of England">King Henry&#160;VI</a>. Stafford acted as a peacemaker during the 1430s, when <a href="/wiki/Humphrey,_Duke_of_Gloucester" title="Humphrey, Duke of Gloucester">Humphrey, Duke of Gloucester</a> vied with <a href="/wiki/Henry_Beaufort" title="Henry Beaufort">Cardinal Beaufort</a> for political supremacy. He took part in the eventual arrest of Gloucester in 1447. He was the King's bodyguard and chief negotiator during <a href="/wiki/Jack_Cade%27s_Rebellion" title="Jack Cade&#39;s Rebellion">Jack Cade's Rebellion</a> of 1450. In 1455 he fought for the King in the first battle of the Wars of the Roses, at <a href="/wiki/First_Battle_of_St_Albans" title="First Battle of St Albans">St Albans</a>, where they were both captured by the <a href="/wiki/House_of_York" title="House of York">Yorkists</a>. He spent the last years of his life attempting to mediate between the Yorkist and Lancastrian factions. <h2 id="mp-tfp-h2" style="margin:0.5em; background:#ddcef2; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #afa3bf; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em"><span id="Today.27s_featured_picture"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Today's_featured_picture">Today's featured picture</span></h2>

<td style="padding:0 6px 0 0">
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Gonepteryx_rhamni" title="Gonepteryx rhamni">Gonepteryx rhamni</a></b></i>, also known as the common brimstone, is a butterfly in the family <a href="/wiki/Pieridae" title="Pieridae">Pieridae</a>. It lives throughout the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic_realm" title="Palearctic realm">Palearctic</a> zone and is commonly found across Europe, Asia and North Africa. The butterfly relies on two species of <a href="/wiki/Rhamnus_(genus)" title="Rhamnus (genus)">buckthorn</a> as hosts for its eggs and larvae which influences its geographic range and distribution, as these plants are commonly found in wetlands. After spending the summer feeding, adults travel to woodland areas to spend seven months <a href="/wiki/Hibernation" title="Hibernation">hibernating</a>. In spring when their host plants have developed, they return to the wetlands to breed and lay eggs. Both the larval and adult forms of the species have protective coloration and behaviour that decreases their chances of being recognised and preyed upon. The adult common brimstone has <a href="/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism" title="Sexual dimorphism">sexual dichromism</a> in its wing coloration and <a href="/wiki/Iridescence" title="Iridescence">iridescence</a>; the male <i>(pictured)</i> has yellow wings and iridescence, while females have greenish-white wings and are not iridescent.
</p>
